Cardio Monitoring

Cardiac monitoring is a way of watching the electrical activity of your heart to ensure it is working normally. Five small stickers, called electrodes, are placed on your body. These are connected either to a small box (a telemetry box) or a large screen on the wall (a wall monitor)!

Medical Treatment

Definition of medical treatment. Medical treatment means the management and care of a patient to combat disease or disorder. Medical treatment includes: All treatment not otherwise excluded (below). Using prescription medications, or use of a non-prescription drug at prescription strength!

Emergency Help

Emergency care refers to immediate medical treatment provided when a medical condition such as illness or injury occurs. Providing patients with life-saving practices such as giving CPR and intravenous medication is emergency care to stabilize them before reaching out to the hospital!

First Aid

First aid refers to medical attention that is usually administered immediately after the injury occurs and at the location where it occurred. It often consists of a one-time, short-term treatment and requires little technology or training to administer.!

Serious Case

In a serious case at a hospital, such as a mass casualty event or a major medical crisis, rapid response teams mobilize to triage and treat patients. Emergency protocols are activated, intensive care units are utilized, and surgical teams may perform life-saving procedures. Communication and coordination are critical amidst heightened urgency.

Emergency Case

During an emergency case in a hospital, such as a cardiac arrest or trauma, rapid response teams swiftly intervene. Medical personnel initiate life-saving measures like CPR, administer medications, and prepare for emergency procedures. Communication is urgent, with clear directives to stabilize the patient for further treatment or transfer to specialized care.
